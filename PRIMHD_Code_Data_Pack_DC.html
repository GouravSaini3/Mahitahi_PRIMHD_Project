This is when a clinician or kaimahi:
-Meets individually with a person
-To assess, support, plan, or review their care

It could include:

-Talking about how the person is doing (assessment)
-Providing therapy or support (treatment)
-Helping with medication
-Creating or updating a care plan
-Reviewing progress
-Preparing for discharge (ending support)

It may or may not include:

Whānau or support people		In a Kaupapa Māori setting, a T53 is more than just a clinical session.

D. Practice Guide for Selecting the Right Breakdown for Kaimahi

This guide helps staff select the most appropriate Activity Breakdown for any interaction. Remember: one interaction can fit multiple activity types — always record the primary purpose of the activity.

Important Practice Guidance for Staff

One Interaction Can Fit Multiple Activity Types. For example, a worker transporting a whaiora to a GP appointment while discussing relapse triggers and rebuilding trust could include: Engagement & Whakawhanaungatanga, Clinical & Health Support, and Social & Practical Support.

The key is: Record the PRIMARY purpose of the activity [Primary Type(Setting),Classification(T-Code),and max time added under primary purpose Breakdown].

If the main focus was…Activity Type / Breakdown
Building trust and connectionEngagement & Whakawhanaungatanga
Goals, assessments, reviewsAssessment, Planning & Review
Health, addiction, treatmentClinical & Health Support
Culture, spirituality, identityCultural, Spiritual & Holistic Wellbeing
Everyday practical helpSocial & Practical Support
Jobs, study, trainingEmployment & Vocational Pathways
Groups and structured activitiesProgramme Delivery & Participation
Coordinating servicesCase Coordination & Service Transitions
Family/community outcomesWhānau & Wider Outcomes

1 Engagement & Whakawhanaungatanga

Building trust, connection, safety, and relationship. The relational foundation that allows support to happen.

e.g. Phone welfare checks, coffee catch-ups, home visits for connection, re-engaging after disengagement, text check-ins.

2 Assessment, Planning & Review

Understanding needs, setting goals, reviewing progress, and planning next steps — including formal and informal assessments.

e.g. Completing assessments, developing support plans, goal setting, risk plan reviews, measuring outcomes.

3 Clinical & Health Support

Health-related interventions, treatment support, symptom management, medication support, and clinical care.

e.g. Medication support, GP appointments, mental health monitoring, relapse prevention, therapy sessions.

4 Cultural, Spiritual & Holistic Wellbeing

Support focused on identity, wairua, tikanga, culture, belonging, meaning, and holistic wellbeing — recognising recovery is more than symptom reduction.

e.g. Karakia, marae visits, cultural reconnection, rongoā Māori, whakapapa exploration, tangihanga support.

5 Social & Practical Support

Helping with daily life needs and practical barriers that affect stability and wellbeing.

e.g. WINZ support, food support, housing applications, budgeting, transport, cleaning support, advocacy.

6 Employment, Education & Vocational Pathways

Support focused on helping people move toward employment, education, volunteering, training, or purposeful activity.

e.g. CV creation, job searching, training enrolments, driver licence support, work readiness, volunteering pathways.

7 Programme Delivery & Participation

Structured programmes, groups, workshops, and organised activities — either facilitating or participating.

e.g. Running recovery groups, life skills programmes, wānanga, psychoeducation groups, recovery workshops.

8 Case Coordination & Service Transitions

Behind-the-scenes coordination ensuring services communicate and transitions happen safely.

e.g. Multi-agency meetings, referrals, coordinating services, transition planning, hospital discharge planning.

9 Whānau & Wider Outcomes

Impacts and support involving whānau, tamariki, relationships, and wider community wellbeing.

e.g. Whānau hui, parenting support, family mediation, rebuilding relationships, strengthening natural supports.

10 Administration

Operational work needed to support service delivery but not direct support itself.

e.g. Writing case notes, emails, reporting, data entry, internal admin, scheduling, filing documentation.

F. Next Steps After Review & Confirmation

Once we receive review and feedback from Directors and Branch Managers on the shared Data Pack, and confirm the PRIMHD activity codes for their services, we will move forward with proposing a new activity template design.

The proposed advanced activity template will:

  • Introduce guided data entry with built-in validation.
  • Ensure a clear distinction between Direct Contact and Attempt to Contact with Whaiora.
  • Provide context-appropriate field visibility.
  • Prevent invalid field combinations and minimise incorrect data entry.

This approach will streamline data capture, improve accuracy, and support consistent reporting across all services.
